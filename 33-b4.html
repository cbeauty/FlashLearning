1. What is Evolution?

Evolution is the change in the inherited characteristics of biological populations over successive generations. The most widely accepted explanation is the theory of natural selection, first proposed by Charles Darwin.

2. Natural Selection

This is the central mechanism of evolution. It can be broken down into key steps:

  1. **Variation:** Individuals within a species show a wide range of variation in their characteristics (due to mutations).
  2. **Competition:** Individuals must compete for resources like food, water, and shelter.
  3. **Survival of the Fittest:** Individuals with characteristics best suited to the environment are more likely to survive and reproduce.
  4. **Inheritance:** The successful genes are passed on to the next generation. Over time, the advantageous characteristics become more common in the population.

3. Adaptation & Competition

An **adaptation** is a feature that helps an organism survive and reproduce in its specific habitat. This can be a structural, functional, or behavioural feature.

Examples of adaptations could include a bird's beak shape for specific food sources, or camouflage to avoid predators.

4. Evidence for Evolution

We know evolution happens because of several key pieces of evidence:

  • **Fossils:** The fossil record shows how different species have changed over geological time.
  • **Homologous Structures:** Similar bone structures in different species (like a human arm and a whale flipper) suggest a common ancestor.
  • **DNA Comparison:** Genetic sequencing allows us to see how closely related different species are.

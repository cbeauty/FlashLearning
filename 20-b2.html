Introduction to the Cell Cycle

The cell cycle is the series of events that take place in a cell leading to its division and duplication (mitosis). This process is essential for **growth**, **repair**, and **asexual reproduction** in multicellular organisms. The cycle has three main stages: interphase, mitosis, and cytokinesis.

Stage 1: Interphase

Growth and DNA Replication

This is the longest stage where the cell spends most of its life. During interphase, the cell prepares for division:

  • The cell grows in size.
  • It increases the number of **sub-cellular structures** (like mitochondria and ribosomes).
  • The DNA replicates to form two identical copies of each chromosome.

Stage 2: Mitosis

Nuclear Division

Mitosis is the process where the replicated chromosomes are separated into two new nuclei. The cell essentially divides its nuclear contents equally. One set of chromosomes is pulled to each end of the cell.

Stage 3: Cytokinesis

Cell Division

This is the final step of the cell cycle. The cytoplasm and cell membrane divide to form two new, separate daughter cells. Each new cell is genetically identical to the original parent cell and has a complete set of chromosomes.

Calculating Magnification (GCSE Students)

A quick guide to mastering magnification calculations in Biology and Physics.

The Formula

The key to calculating magnification is a simple formula that can be rearranged to find any of the three variables: magnification, image size, or actual size.

Magnification = Image Size ÷ Actual Size

Remember the triangle to rearrange the formula easily:

  • To find Actual Size: Image Size ÷ Magnification
  • To find Image Size: Magnification × Actual Size

Units, Units, Units!

It is crucial that all units are the same before you start calculating!

In science, especially when looking at cells under a microscope, you'll often need to convert between millimeters (mm) and micrometers (μm).

1 mm = 1000 μm (micrometers)

To convert mm to μm, multiply by 1000. To convert μm to mm, divide by 1000.

Worked Example

Question: An image of a plant cell has a diameter of 20 mm. The magnification of the image is ×850. What is the actual diameter of the cell in micrometers (μm)?

Step 1: Rearrange the formula to find the actual size:

Actual Size = Image Size ÷ Magnification

Step 2: Ensure units are consistent. Convert the image size from mm to μm:

20 mm × 1000 = 20,000 μm

Step 3: Substitute the values into the formula:

Actual Size = 20,000 μm ÷ 850

Step 4: Calculate the result:

Actual Size ≈ 23.53 μm

Sex-Linked Genetic Disorders

A GCSE Biology Revision Guide

Understanding Sex Linkage

In humans, we have 23 pairs of chromosomes. 22 pairs are **autosomes** (non-sex chromosomes), and one pair are the **sex chromosomes** (X and Y).

  • Females have two X chromosomes (XX).
  • Males have one X and one Y chromosome (XY).

Sex-linked disorders are caused by faulty alleles located on these sex chromosomes, most commonly the X chromosome. The Y chromosome is much smaller and carries fewer genes.

Inheritance Patterns

The unique inheritance pattern means males are more likely to suffer from X-linked recessive disorders because they only have one X chromosome. Females have a "backup" X, which usually masks the effect of a recessive allele, making them a **carrier**.

Key Facts:

  • Males (XY): If they inherit a faulty X chromosome, they will have the disorder.
  • Females (XX): Need two faulty X chromosomes to express the disorder. If they inherit one, they are a carrier and are usually unaffected.
  • Males inherit their X chromosome from their mother and their Y from their father.
  • Females inherit one X from each parent.

Common Examples

Red-Green Colour Blindness

This is a classic example of an X-linked recessive disorder.

  • Sufferers find it difficult to distinguish between red and green shades.
  • It affects about 8% of men but only about 1% of women.
  • Genotype notation uses the X and Y chromosomes with superscript alleles (e.g., XB for normal vision, Xb for colour blindness). A carrier female is XBXb.

Haemophilia

Haemophilia is another X-linked recessive disorder where the blood does not clot properly.

  • Minor injuries can cause continuous bleeding due to the lack of necessary clotting factors.
  • Historically known for affecting European royal families.
  • It primarily affects males.

Punnett Squares (Genetic Diagrams)

You need to be able to use Punnett squares to predict inheritance probabilities. Remember to include the sex chromosomes (X and Y) with the alleles as superscripts.

Example Cross: A carrier female (XBXb) and an unaffected male (XBY)

Possible offspring genotypes and phenotypes:

X&supB; (Male gamete) Y (Male gamete)
X&supB; (Female gamete) X&supB;X&supB; (Normal female) X&supB;Y (Normal male)
X&supb; (Female gamete) X&supB;X&supb; (Carrier female) X&supb;Y (Affected male)

In this cross, there is a 25% chance of a male offspring having colour blindness.

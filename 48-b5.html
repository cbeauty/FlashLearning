The Cardiovascular System

Introduction

The cardiovascular system, also known as the circulatory system, is an organ system composed of the heart, blood vessels, and blood. These components work together to transport essential substances around the body, much like a complex delivery network. This page will cover the structure, function, and common diseases related to this vital system.

Key Functions

The main functions of the cardiovascular system include:

  • Delivery: Transporting oxygen, nutrients, hormones, and other important substances to all body cells and organs.
  • Removal: Removing waste products such as carbon dioxide and lactic acid from the body tissues.
  • Protection: Playing a role in the body's defense against disease and infection.
  • Regulation: Helping to maintain a stable body temperature.

Anatomy of the Heart

The heart is the muscular organ that acts as the pump, ensuring the continuous flow of blood through the vessels.

Image: Diagram showing the key parts of the human heart (e.g., atria, ventricles, valves).

Key components include:

  • Right Atrium & Right Ventricle: Receive deoxygenated blood from the body and pump it to the lungs.
  • Left Atrium & Left Ventricle: Receive oxygenated blood from the lungs and pump it to the rest of the body. The left ventricle has a thicker wall as it needs to pump blood further.
  • Valves: Prevent the backflow of blood, ensuring it flows in one direction only.

Blood Vessels

Blood vessels are tubes that direct blood throughout the body. There are three main types:

  1. Arteries: Carry oxygenated blood away from the heart to the body (except the pulmonary artery). They have thick, muscular walls to withstand high pressure.
  2. Veins: Carry deoxygenated blood back to the heart (except the pulmonary vein). They have thinner walls and contain valves to assist blood flow against gravity.
  3. Capillaries: Tiny, thin-walled vessels that allow for the exchange of materials (oxygen, nutrients, waste) between the blood and body cells.

The Double Circulatory System

Humans have a double circulatory system, meaning blood passes through the heart twice for each complete circuit of the body.

  • Pulmonary Circuit: Heart – Lungs – Heart (oxygenates blood).
  • Systemic Circuit: Heart – Body – Heart (delivers oxygen and nutrients).

Cardiovascular Diseases (CVD)

CVD refers to conditions affecting the heart or blood vessels. These often arise from the buildup of fatty deposits in arteries, a condition called atherosclerosis.

Risk factors for developing coronary heart disease (a common CVD) are an important part of the GCSE biology syllabus.

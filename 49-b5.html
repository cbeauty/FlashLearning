The Cardiovascular System

Your guide to the heart, blood vessels, and blood for GCSE Biology.

Introduction

The cardiovascular system (also known as the circulatory system) is a vital organ system composed of the heart, blood vessels, and blood. Its primary function is to transport substances around the body, including oxygen, nutrients, hormones, and waste products.

The Heart: The Muscular Pump

The heart is a large muscular pump divided into four chambers: the left and right atria (where blood collects) and the left and right ventricles (which pump blood out).

Key Parts:

  • **Right side:** Receives deoxygenated blood from the body and pumps it to the lungs.
  • **Left side:** Receives oxygenated blood from the lungs and pumps it around the rest of the body.
  • **Septum:** The muscular wall separating the left and right sides of the heart.
  • **Valves (Tricuspid, Bicuspid/Mitral, Semilunar):** Prevent the backflow of blood.

Blood Vessels: The Transport Network

There are three main types of blood vessels, each with a specific function:

  1. **Arteries:** Carry blood away from the heart, typically at high pressure. They have thick, muscular walls.
  2. **Veins:** Carry blood towards the heart, at lower pressure. They have thinner walls and contain valves to prevent backflow.
  3. **Capillaries:** Tiny, thin-walled vessels that allow for the exchange of materials (oxygen, nutrients, waste) between the blood and body cells.

Blood: The Transport Fluid

Blood itself is a tissue made of several components:

  • **Red blood cells:** Transport oxygen using haemoglobin.
  • **White blood cells:** Involved in the immune response and fighting infection.
  • **Platelets:** Cell fragments involved in blood clotting.
  • **Plasma:** The liquid component that carries everything else (cells, nutrients, waste, hormones).

Cardiovascular Diseases (CVD)

CVD refers to conditions affecting the heart or blood vessels. A common cause is the build-up of fatty deposits (atherosclerosis) in arteries, which can lead to blockages, heart attacks, or strokes. Risk factors for coronary heart disease are an important topic in GCSE biology.
